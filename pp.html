Let’s make sharing music easier


The way we listen to our favorite songs completely changed with streaming services like Youtube, Spotify, and Apple music. With all these apps consolidating all of the songs we love in one place, why not make sharing and discussing them just as easy?

An opportunity space


While most of us use streaming apps like Spotify every day, we each have all sorts of different ways to share our new favorite songs with our friends. I personally have GroupMe's with a few friends where we share and talk about new music. While this method works, I feel this is a huge opportunity that Spotify and other music streaming apps miss out by not allowing users to share and discuss music easily within the app.

Research


Through my research, I found that almost all of my friends and peers at U.Va do have some sort of group or method for sharing music. Through a quick survey on our class FB page, I found for sharing music with friends, most people sent music through text or IM groups such as Facebook messenger, Instagram direct messages, and GroupMe. Over 90% of my peers felt that music introduced to them by their friends was better than suggestions from Youtube or Spotify.

This quote I found from a GQ article sums up my findings nicely:

"Occasionally I let the robots tell me what to hear, most often SoundCloud autoplay after I’ve been listening to something I like, or a peek at Spotify’s Discover Weekly. I prefer humans, obviously."

- Jillian Mapes, Pitchfork Senior Editor

So how can we allow peer sharing on Spotify?

The solution


Introducing Spotify Groups! This is a concept I'm currently pursuing which would act as a group/direct messaging feature. Users can send songs, playlists, discuss Lil Wayne's latest album, etc. all within the Spotify application.
