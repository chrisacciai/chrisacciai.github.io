Let’s make sharing music easier


Listening to music has never been easier with streaming services like Youtube, Spotify, and Apple music. With all these apps consolidating all of the songs we love in one place, why not make sharing and discussing them just as easy?











An opportunity space


While most of us use streaming apps like Spotify every day, we each have all sorts of different ways to share our new favorite songs with our friends. I personally have a GroupMe's with a few friends where we share and talk about new music. While this method works, I feel this is a huge opportunity that Spotify and other music streaming apps miss out by not allowing users to share and discuss music easily within the app.











Research


Through my research, I found that almost all of my friends and peers at U.Va do have some sort of group or method for sharing music. Through a quick survey on our class FB page, I found for sharing music with friends, most people sent music through text or IM groups such as Facebook messenger, Instagram direct messages, and GroupMe. Over 90% of my peers felt that music introduced to them by their friends was better than suggestions from Youtube or Spotify.



This quote I found from a GQ article sums up my findings nicely:

"Occasionally I let the robots tell me what to hear, most often SoundCloud autoplay after I’ve been listening to something I like, or a peek at Spotify’s Discover Weekly. I prefer humans, obviously."

- Jillian Mapes, Pitchfork Senior Editor







So how can we allow peer sharing on Spotify?

Iterations, Iterations


Since several teams wanted to see the application completed, a lot of my time was devoted to the actual development of my design. However, design is never finished and there are several stylistic and functional changes I began implementing and testing once I finished the beta version. Although the original design was to help Kenya create a specific type of dashboard, with new teams looking to use the application I began expirimenting with a feature to allow users to add and delete their own custom charts.






Takeaways


Make your ideas heard:

What started out as a small project to help make Kenya's day easier, expanded in scope to affect multiple divisions and teams.

The user is always number one:

I constanty met with Kenya to get her thoughts, input, and concerns during every stage of contextual inquiry, design, prototyping, implemention, and testing. By working with her so closely I was able to build a functional and intuitive application that many people were interested in using.

The complex solution is not always the best:

When researching the problem, I took a look at several existing applications such as Tableau and Microsoft's Power BI. While incredibly useful tools, they were overkill for Kenya's simple but tedious task. I decided to keep the dashboard very basic and only include the necessary functionality (I like to think of it as a healthy step up from Excel).
